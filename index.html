Our Mission

This summer, I began designing and 3D-printing custom toy cars to donate to children in underserved communities. My goal? To make STEM and design accessible, fun, and meaningful—one toy at a time.

My Journey

Getting Started: I used Tinkercad to design a Formula 1-inspired toy car, learning modeling, safety, and child-friendly scaling. I printed my first prototypes at the Brampton Library Makerspace.

Facing Challenges: Each print took 3 hours and printers were busy. I scaled models down 55% and optimized print supports to reduce time and waste.

Donation Day: After emailing King Academy Child Care Centre, I dropped off 3 packaged cars. Seeing kids play with them reminded me why I started.

Sustainability: I minimized plastic by designing cross-hatch interiors, reinforcing weak points, and filing edges. The result: lightweight, safe, and durable toy cars built with care.

About the Project

“Driven to Give” started as a passion for cars, design, and helping others. With each 3D-printed toy, I’ve learned about CAD, materials, sustainability, and what it means to give back through engineering. I’m continuing to expand this initiative by creating printable kits and STEM prompt cards for kids.
