Our Mission

This summer, I began designing and 3D-printing custom toy cars to donate to children in underserved communities. My goal? To make STEM and design accessible, fun, and meaningful—one toy at a time.

My Journey

This project began with a simple idea: combine my love for Formula 1 and design with a way to help kids smile. I learned how to use Tinkercad to 3D model a car, and printed my first prototype using the Brampton Library’s Makerspace. Over time, I scaled the model, refined it, and ensured it was safe and fun for children.

I faced real obstacles—each car took nearly 3 hours to print, and the printer was often booked. Still, I managed to create and package 3 working models and reached out to King Academy Child Care Centre to donate them. After confirming by email, I officially delivered the cars, seeing them bring joy to real kids.

I also reflected on every aspect of the design: how to make it lightweight and safe, how to reduce plastic waste during printing, and how to cater the form factor to young children. Using techniques like cross-hatching and reinforced wings, I minimized material use and maximized durability. With bright colors, smoothed edges, and functional wheels, each car represents thoughtful engineering.

From rough sketches and CAD software to printed models in kids' hands, this journey taught me the value of persistence, problem-solving, and purpose-driven design.

About the Project

“Driven to Give” started as a passion for cars, design, and helping others. With each 3D-printed toy, I’ve learned about CAD, materials, and what it means to give back through engineering. I’m continuing to expand this initiative by developing printable kits and STEM-based learning cards for kids to explore how their cars work.
