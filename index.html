Our Mission

This summer, I began designing and 3D-printing custom toy cars to donate to children in underserved communities. My goal? To make STEM and design accessible, fun, and meaningful—one toy at a time.

My Journey

Getting Started: I used Tinkercad to design a Formula 1-inspired toy car, learning about modeling, safety, and scaling for child-friendly use. I used the Brampton Library Makerspace to print my first prototypes using yellow filament.

Challenges: Each print took 3 hours, and the printer worked on a first-come-first-serve basis. I had to scale down the design by 55% and optimize support placement to save time and material while maintaining structure.

Donation Day: After emailing King Academy Child Care Centre, I donated 3 packaged cars. Seeing the cars bring joy to real kids reminded me why I started this in the first place.

Sustainability & Design: I minimized waste by reinforcing weak points and using cross-hatch interiors to reduce plastic. I filed sharp edges and kept weight light for safety. The result: a durable, safe, and fun toy car built with care.

About the Project

“Driven to Give” started as a passion for cars, design, and helping others. With each 3D-printed toy, I’ve learned about CAD, materials, and what it means to give back through engineering. I’m continuing to expand this initiative by developing printable kits and STEM-based learning cards for kids to explore how their cars work.
